## Who We Are

RLTech LLC is a minority-owned small business headquartered in Aurora, Colorado. We are a specialized cybersecurity and Identity and Access Management (IAM) firm focused on the Microsoft security ecosystem — specifically Microsoft Entra ID, Zero Trust architecture, and AI identity security.

RLTech was founded by Dominick Skelton with a clear thesis: most federal agencies and government contractors have a serious identity problem. They have too many over-privileged accounts, inconsistent MFA enforcement, no privileged access governance, and Zero Trust strategies that exist on paper but not in production. RLTech exists to fix that — with a focused, cost-effective, documentation-driven approach that large integrators can't match at this price point.

## Competitive Positioning

### Why RLTech instead of a large integrator?

Large integrators (Booz Allen Hamilton, Leidos, SAIC, Accenture Federal) bill IAM resources at $175–$300+/hour fully loaded. Their overhead structures require layers of program management, benched labor cost recovery, and corporate G&A markup that clients pay for whether they need it or not.

RLTech's fully-loaded T&M rates run $95–$225/hour — 15–40% below large integrators — while delivering the same Microsoft-certified expertise. The cost advantage is structural, not quality-based: no corporate overhead, no benched labor, no unnecessary program management layers.

For task-based and micro-purchase engagements specifically, RLTech is purpose-built. Fixed-price packages start at $3,500 and are scoped to deliver a defined written report and a 1-hour briefing — something a large integrator cannot compete on administratively or commercially.

### Why RLTech instead of a generalist MSP?

Most managed service providers (MSPs) and IT consulting firms treat IAM as a line item inside a broader IT services portfolio. RLTech's entire practice is IAM and identity security. This means deeper knowledge of Microsoft Entra edge cases, compliance framework nuances (NIST 800-63 Level of Assurance mapping, CISA ZTMM maturity scoring), and federal procurement requirements (micro-purchase eligibility, simplified acquisition, FAR compliance clauses).

### Why RLTech for subcontracting/teaming?

Primes on federal IT programs often win work that includes identity security scope they don't have deep bench strength for. RLTech is designed to fill that gap — as a subcontractor or teaming partner — with a minority-owned small business certification that contributes to the prime's diversity spend goals. Fast ramp-up, defined deliverables, and minimal management overhead make RLTech an easy integration for program teams.

**Phase 4 — Scale through teaming and staff augmentation**

- Position AI Workload Identity Security as a forward-looking specialization
- Non-human identity governance is the fastest-growing unaddressed risk surface in enterprise environments
- Most competitors (including large integrators) are not yet specialized here

## Business Philosophy

RLTech operates on a few core principles:

1. **Depth over breadth** — Specialization in IAM and identity security produces better outcomes than generalism. We do not try to be everything to every client.

2. **Documentation-driven delivery** — Every engagement produces written, auditable deliverables. This is non-negotiable for federal work and good practice everywhere. Clients should be able to defend every configuration change to an auditor.

3. **Framework alignment** — Solutions are grounded in real frameworks (NIST, CISA, Microsoft), not proprietary methodologies that create lock-in. Clients own the knowledge.

4. **Small business cost advantage** — RLTech's pricing reflects its actual cost structure. Clients aren't paying for overhead they don't need.

5. **Long-term relationships** — The goal is not a one-time engagement but a trusted advisory relationship where RLTech grows with the client's security maturity over time.
